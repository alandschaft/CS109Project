Background

Standard medical treatment does not always work perfectly well for each patient. These patients wish to be informed about innovative new treatments that are tested in clinical trials. Moreover, doctors may whish to inform their patients about clinical trials that are relevant to their health problem and advise them about the risks and opportunities related to these trials.

Descriptions of clinical trials are publicly available, for example in clinicaltrials.gov. However it is a hard task to query those descriptions and quickly find clinical trials that are relevant to a patient with a certain condition, as they contain unstructured data. Particularly inclusion and exclusion criteria are written in natural language as it is important for the publishers of clinical trials (research hospitals, pharmaceuticals) to describe them in their own words.

Our mission is helping patients and their doctors on one side and the research hospitals and pharmaceuticals on the other side to find clinical trials match.

Particularly, we would like to build foundations for a search application for clinical trials that will allow the user to type a free text search term, for example “seizure” and then ask the user a minimal set of simple multiple choice questions. Each answer will narrow down the list of relevant clinical trials. For example, searching for “seizure” in clinicaltrials.gov returns a list of more than a thousand clinical trials. After answering a minimal set of questions, the user shall be left with less than 10 possibilities.

Our main focus in this project is on Inclusion and Exclusion criteria for clinical trials because these are the most important parts, clinically, for the patients, their doctors and the facilitators of the clinical trials.

Dataset

The dataset consists of publicly available clinical trials data from clinicaltrials.gov. This site is a service of the U.S. National Institutes of Health that has a database of publicly and privately supported clinical studies.

For the project we downloaded all clinical trials data that matched the search term "seizure" so we could work with a more manageable amount of data on a specific subject. This gave us 1,347 clinical trials to work with.
